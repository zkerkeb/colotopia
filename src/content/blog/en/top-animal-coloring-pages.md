## Tips for the Best Printable Animal Coloring Experience

To make sure your printed pages look great and are easy to work with:

- **Use cardstock or heavy paper** (at least 120 gsm) to prevent marker bleed-through.
- **Print in high quality mode** for crisp, clean outlines.
- **Select "Fit to Page"** in your printer settings so the design fills the sheet properly.
- **Print extras** -- children often want to color the same favorite page multiple times, and backups are handy for experiments or mistakes.

### What coloring tools work best for animal pages?

Colored pencils are ideal because they allow for blending and shading, which is important for realistic animal fur and feathers. Crayons work well for younger children. Markers produce bold, vibrant results but require heavier paper to avoid bleed-through.
